Why this works Why the cat thinks this works

The agent does not predict football better than the market. It converts market odds into score probabilities and selects the result with the highest expected Kicktipp points. The cat does not claim to see the future. It simply sniffs the market odds, converts them into score probabilities and chooses the bowl with the most expected tuna.

Why so many 1:0 and 0:1 tips? Why so many 1:0 and 0:1 bowls?
Kicktipp awards 2 points for the correct tendency alone. A narrow win like 1:0 (home favorite) or 0:1 (away favorite) captures many outcomes: exact hit (4 pts), correct goal difference (3 pts), and any one-goal win in that direction (2 pts). Higher scores such as 2:0 are more precise but miss more real results — so EV optimization often prefers minimal winning scores, not because the model expects 1:0 every time. Kicktipp gives 2 treats for the right winner alone. A tight score like 1:0 or 0:1 matches many real naps (exact, goal diff, one-goal win) and maximizes expected tuna. The cat is optimizing treats, not predicting a 1:0 conspiracy.
